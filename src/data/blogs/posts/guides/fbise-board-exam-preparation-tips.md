## ‚úÖ Step-by-Step Preparation & Performance Plan
Follow these phases from start to finish.

### 1. Foundation & Planning (Start Early)
*   **Self-Assessment:** Take a practice test to honestly assess which subjects need more focus.
*   **Batch Your Subjects:** Group subjects to avoid burnout. Pair a hard subject (e.g., Physics) with an easier one (e.g., English) and rotate daily.
*   **Create a Realistic Schedule:** Block out 4-5 hours of focused study daily in your schedule. Prioritize difficult subjects when your mind is freshest.

### 2. Core Study & Practice
*   **Master Past Papers:** They are your most important tool. Solving them helps you recognize question patterns and identify frequently tested topics.
*   **Focus on SLOs:** Since 2022, FBISE exams focus on Student Learning Outcomes (SLOs). Ensure you use the latest SLO-based model papers and past papers (2022 onwards).
*   **Prioritize Understanding:** Avoid rote memorization. Focus on understanding concepts to answer application-based questions.

### 3. Exam Hall Strategy (Maximize Your Score)
*   **Time Allocation:** Before writing, note each question's marks. Allocate time proportionally and stick to it to avoid missing questions.
*   **Impeccable Presentation:** This creates a strong impression:
    *   Use a blue pen for text and a blue marker for drawing margins and underlining headings.
    *   Write question numbers clearly in the centre.
    *   Highlight key terms, definitions, and formulas with a marker.
    *   Use diagrams, flowcharts, and tables wherever possible.
    *   Never scribble. If you make a mistake, put brackets around it and a small cross above each bracket.

## üîç After the Exams: Results & Next Steps
*   **Official Result Portals:** Your Roll Number is required to check results. Avoid unofficial sites and only use your board's official website.
*   **Result Verification:** If your score is significantly below expectations, boards offer a re-checking or re-counting service for a fee, followed by a more detailed paper re-evaluation.
*   **Supplementary Exams:** If you fail a subject, you can retake it in the annual supplementary exams (usually held 2-3 months after the main results).
