The history of the rivalry is defined by long periods of hiatus and dramatic resumptions, dictated by political relations.

## History and Political Intertwining
*   **The Beginning (1952):** Pakistan received Test status in 1952 and played its first Test series against India, hosted by India. This marked the official start of the rivalry.
*   **The Hiatuses:** The two major wars between the nations in 1965 and 1971 caused cricket ties to be suspended for over a decade (1962 to 1977). Cricket has always served as a barometer for bilateral relations, resuming during periods of diplomatic warming (e.g., in 1978 and 2004) and being suspended during times of high tension.
*   **Cricket Diplomacy (2004):** The most famous resumption occurred in 2004 when India toured Pakistan for the first time in 14 years. This tour, initiated as a peace process, was lauded globally as an example of cricket diplomacy and led to a brief, beautiful period of bilateral tours.
*   **The Modern Era:** Since 2007, bilateral series (home and away tours) have largely stopped due to geopolitical strains. Today, the teams only meet in multi-nation tournaments governed by the ICC (World Cups, Champions Trophy) and the ACC (Asia Cup), guaranteeing limited, but high-stakes, encounters.
