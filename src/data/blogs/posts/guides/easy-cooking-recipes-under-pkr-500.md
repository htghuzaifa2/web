I’ve always enjoyed cooking on a budget — it’s like a little creative challenge. Here are some simple recipes under PKR 500 that feel hearty, tasty, and easy to prepare.

## 1. Vegetable Pulao
A classic and affordable meal.
*   **Ingredients:** 1 cup basmati rice, carrot, potato, peas, onion, green chili, cumin, spices.
*   **Instructions:** Sauté onions and spices. Add veggies, then rice, and cook until done.
*   **Cost Estimate:** ~PKR 250 for 2–3 servings.

## 2. Chana Masala (Chickpea Curry)
High in protein, simple, and filling.
*   **Ingredients:** Boiled chickpeas, onion, tomato, green chili, spices.
*   **Instructions:** Make a masala with onions and tomatoes, add chickpeas, simmer.
*   **Cost Estimate:** ~PKR 200–250 for 2 servings.

## 3. Aloo Keema (Potato & Minced Meat)
Affordable, flavorful, and comforting.
*   **Ingredients:** 200g minced beef/chicken, 2 potatoes, onion, tomato, spices.
*   **Instructions:** Brown meat with onions, add potatoes and tomatoes, simmer.
*   **Cost Estimate:** ~PKR 450 for 2–3 servings.

## 4. Egg & Vegetable Stir-Fry
Quick, protein-packed, and easy.
*   **Ingredients:** 3 eggs, carrot, peas, capsicum, onion.
*   **Instructions:** Sauté veggies, pour beat eggs over them, stir until cooked.
*   **Cost Estimate:** ~PKR 150–200 for 2 servings.

## 5. Lentil Soup (Daal Shorba)
Simple, nutritious, and comforting.
*   **Ingredients:** Masoor dal, carrot, tomato, onion, spices.
*   **Instructions:** Boil lentils with veggies until soft. Blend lightly.
*   **Cost Estimate:** ~PKR 150–200 for 2 servings.

### Quick Tips for Budget Cooking
*   Buy seasonal vegetables.
*   Use local staples like rice and lentils.
*   Cook in batches.

### A Little Reflection
Cooking on a budget doesn’t mean compromising taste. With simple ingredients and creativity, you can make meals that feel warm and homey.
