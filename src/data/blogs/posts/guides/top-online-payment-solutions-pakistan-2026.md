Setting up payments used to feel like a puzzle. Now, we have solid options. Whether you run a store or freelance, here’s what works in 2026.

## 1. EasyPaisa – Everyday Local Payments
*   **Pros:** Trusted by millions, no big setup fees, works well for local e-commerce.
*   **Cons:** Mostly local; international clients can't use it directly.

## 2. JazzCash – Big Wallet, Big Reach
*   **Pros:** Widespread adoption, supports wallet + card transactions.
*   **Cons:** International payments aren't native.

## 3. PayFast – Local Gateway with Card Support
*   **Pros:** Accepts cards + wallets, licensed by State Bank, works with Shopify/WooCommerce.
*   **Cons:** Merchant onboarding is stricter than wallets.

## 4. XPay – Modern & Conversion-Friendly
*   **Pros:** Smooth on-site checkout (no redirects), supports BNPL (Buy Now, Pay Later) and cards.
*   **Cons:** Newer, so check integration support for your specific platform.

## 5. SHARE – Payment Links & International Help
*   **Pros:** No full store needed (great for social selling), supports international clients via payment links.
*   **Cons:** best for simple workflows, not a full gateway.

## 6. Bank Gateways (MCB, Alfa, HBL, UBL)
*   **Pros:** Full card acceptance (local & global), robust reporting. Ideal for scaling businesses.
*   **Cons:** Onboarding takes longer and requires more documentation.

### Final Thoughts — What Should You Choose?
*   **Local Audience:** EasyPaisa, JazzCash, PayFast.
*   **Professional Checkout w/ Cards:** PayFast, XPay.
*   **Freelance/International:** SHARE or Bank Gateways.
*   **Virtual Cards:** SadaPay, NayaPay (great for managing your own expenses).
