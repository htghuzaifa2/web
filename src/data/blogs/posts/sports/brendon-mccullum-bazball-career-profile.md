## üß† The Coach: Exporting the "Bazball" Revolution

After retiring in 2019, McCullum quickly moved into coaching, where his philosophy found a new home. In 2022, in a bold move, the England and Wales Cricket Board appointed him as the head coach of a struggling England Test team.

### The Birth of "Bazball"

Partnering with captain Ben Stokes, McCullum did more than change tactics; he changed the entire mindset of English Test cricket. He encouraged complete freedom, unwavering aggression, and a disregard for fear of failure. This approach, instantly nicknamed "Bazball" by the media, led to a stunning turnaround. England won 10 of their first 11 Tests under McCullum and Stokes, chasing down seemingly impossible targets with breathtaking bravado.

### Philosophy and Current Challenges

McCullum's coaching ethos mirrors his captaincy: building a positive environment where players are empowered to be their best, aggressive selves. While the initial results were spectacular, the approach has faced stern tests against the world's best teams like Australia and India. Recent Ashes struggles have led to scrutiny, but McCullum remains committed to his long-term contract and philosophy, believing it's the only way for England to achieve sustained success.
