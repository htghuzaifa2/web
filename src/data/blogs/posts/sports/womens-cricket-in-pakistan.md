Hey there, cricket fans. Let's talk about something important and, honestly, a bit frustrating for many of us: the state of women's cricket in Pakistan. We love the game, but if we're being real, watching our women's team sometimes feels like watching a talented but under-prepared boxer step into the ring.

They've got heart, no doubt. But the results? They've been tough. Finishing at the bottom of the 2025 T20 World Cup without a win is a hard pill to swallow. We've never beaten the top teams like Australia, England, or New Zealand, and our overall record is a struggle. For a nation that breathes cricket, why is this the case?

The reasons aren't a mystery. For years, the women's game just didn't get the same fuel as the men's. Less money, fewer facilities, inconsistent coaching, and let's be honest, some old-fashioned societal hurdles that made it hard for girls to even pick up a bat. Without a strong domestic league like the WPL or the Hundred that other countries have, our players missed out on that crucial, high-pressure experience.

But here's where the story gets interesting. Are things changing? Is there a light at the end of a very long tunnel? Let's look at what's actually happening on the ground.
