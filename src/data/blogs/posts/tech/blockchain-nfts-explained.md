| Feature | Blockchain (The Foundation) | NFTs (The Application) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |

| **Key Trait** | **Immutability & Decentralization:** Records can't be altered and are maintained by a network, not a single authority. | **Non-fungibility & Scarcity:** Each token is one-of-a-kind and cannot be exchanged like-for-like. |
| **Main Function** | Provides a secure, transparent, and tamper-proof system for recording data (like transactions or agreements). | Acts as a verifiable digital certificate of ownership and authenticity for a unique item, digital or physical. |

## üîó How They Work Together
Think of blockchain as a public, unchangeable record book. When an NFT is created or "minted," a permanent entry is made in this record book. This entry contains essential information:
1.  A unique identifier (like a serial number) for the NFT.
2.  A record of ownership, which updates whenever the NFT is sold or transferred.
3.  A link to the digital asset it represents (like a JPG or video file).

This process uses smart contracts‚Äîself-executing agreements written in code that live on the blockchain. They automatically handle the rules, like transferring ownership when payment is received.

## üí° Common Uses for NFTs
While digital art popularized NFTs, their use has expanded:
*   **Digital Collectibles:** Art, music, video clips (like NBA highlights), and memes.
*   **Gaming:** Ownership of in-game items, characters, or virtual land.
*   **Memberships & Tickets:** Event tickets or club memberships that can't be forged.
*   **Real-World Assets:** Tokenizing physical items like real estate or luxury goods to simplify proof of ownership.

## ‚ö†Ô∏è Practical Considerations and Risks
If you're interested in buying or creating NFTs, here's what you should know:

*   **You Need a Crypto Wallet:** To interact with NFT marketplaces, you'll need a digital wallet to store cryptocurrency (usually Ethereum) and your NFT assets.
*   **Marketplaces are the Gateway:** Platforms like OpenSea, Rarible, and NBA Top Shot are where most NFTs are bought, sold, and created.
*   **Understand the Major Risks:**
    *   **High Volatility:** NFT values can fluctuate wildly based on speculation and demand.
    *   **Scams & Fraud:** The space has risks like counterfeit NFTs, "rug pulls" (where developers abandon a project), and phishing attempts.
    *   **Environmental Impact:** Some blockchains (like earlier versions of Ethereum) use energy-intensive processes, though many are transitioning to more efficient systems.
